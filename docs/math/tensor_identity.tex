\section*{Divergence of a Symmetric Tensor}

For a symmetric second-rank tensor $T^{\mu\nu}$, the covariant divergence can be written in terms of coordinate derivatives and metric derivatives.

Starting from the general identity:
\begin{equation}
\nabla_\mu T^{\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{-g}} \partial_\mu \left(\sqrt{-g} \, T^{\mu\nu}\right) + \Gamma^\nu_{\mu\lambda} T^{\mu\lambda}
\end{equation}

For a \textbf{symmetric} tensor $T^{\mu\lambda} = T^{\lambda\mu}$, the Christoffel symbol contraction simplifies:
\begin{equation}
\Gamma^\nu_{\mu\lambda} T^{\mu\lambda} = \frac{1}{2} T^{\mu\lambda} g^{\nu\sigma} \partial_\sigma g_{\mu\lambda}
\end{equation}

Therefore, the covariant divergence becomes:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\nabla_\mu T^{\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{-g}} \partial_\mu \left(\sqrt{-g} \, T^{\mu\nu}\right) + \frac{1}{2} T^{\mu\lambda} g^{\nu\sigma} \partial_\sigma g_{\mu\lambda}}
\end{equation}

Equivalently, for the coordinate divergence of the tensor density:
\begin{equation}
\partial_\mu \left(\sqrt{-g} \, T^{\mu\nu}\right) = \sqrt{-g} \left( \nabla_\mu T^{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2} T^{\mu\lambda} g^{\nu\sigma} \partial_\sigma g_{\mu\lambda} \right)
\end{equation}

This identity is useful in general relativity for writing $\nabla_\mu T^{\mu\nu} = 0$ in a form suitable for numerical conservation laws, where the metric derivative term acts as a geometric source.
